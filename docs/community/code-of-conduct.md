# Code of Conduct

KBeacon is a small open source project. The goal is to keep collaboration respectful, technically rigorous, and welcoming.

## Expected behavior

Participants are expected to:

- communicate with empathy and respect;
- assume good intent, while still addressing harmful behavior;
- give and receive constructive technical feedback;
- keep reviews focused on code, design, documentation, operations, and user impact;
- be patient with new contributors;
- avoid sharing private cluster names, Secret names, tokens, customer data, or other sensitive information in public issues.

## Unacceptable behavior

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks;
- discriminatory language or behavior;
- sexualized language or imagery;
- sustained disruption of discussions;
- publishing private information without consent;
- intentionally exposing credentials, Secret values, or sensitive metadata;
- bad-faith contributions that knowingly weaken user safety or security.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove comments, close issues, block users, or restrict participation when behavior harms the project or its users.

If you experience or observe unacceptable behavior, contact a maintainer privately. Security-sensitive reports should follow SECURITY.md.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to KBeacon repositories, issues, pull requests, discussions, documentation, release artifacts, and community spaces managed by project maintainers.
